The concept of a mineral system is well established but poorly defined. The MineralSystem class is a placeholder for further work.<br/><br/>The process of mineralisation (magmatic – fluid/gas – mechanical) can lead to a single deposit or a range of deposits of differing “deposit types”.<br/><br/>The complete system is described by a number of workers as a cycle from ore generation, transport, deposition in traps and residual outflow. <br/><br/>It is therefore possible to describe a mineral system as having a number of characteristics.<br/><br/>It is an <b>event</b> occurring over a period of time.<br/>It involves mineral transport by either magmatic, fluid/gas, or sedimentary processes.<br/>Systems have distinctive characteristics (fluid chemistry etc)<br/>Systems may include a number of deposit types (traps).<br/><br/>
