-- Name --<br /></p><p>Abstract building unit<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>-- Definition --<br /></p><p>Abstract spatial object type grouping the semantic properties of building units.<br /></p><p>A BuildingUnit is a subdivision of Building with its own lockable access from the outside or from a common area (i.e. not from another BuildingUnit), which is atomic, functionally independent, and may be separately sold, rented out, inherited, etc.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>-- Description --<br /></p><p>Building units are spatial objects aimed at subdividing buildings and/or building parts into smaller parts that are treated as seperate entities in daily life. A building unit is homogeneous, regarding management aspects.<br /></p><p>EXAMPLES: It may be e.g. an apartment in a condominium, a terraced house, or a shop inside a shopping arcade.<br /></p><p>NOTE 1: According to national regulations, a building unit may be a flat, a cellar, a garage or set of a flat, a cellar and a garage.<br /></p><p>NOTE 2: According to national regulation, a building that is one entity for daily life (typically, a single family house) may be considered as a Building composed of one BuildingUnit or as a Building composed of zero BuildingUnit.<br /></p>
