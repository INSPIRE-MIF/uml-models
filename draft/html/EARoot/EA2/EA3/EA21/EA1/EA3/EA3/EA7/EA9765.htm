Notes: -- Definition --<br /></p><p>Anode.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>-- Description --<br /></p><p>An <i>anode </i>is a feature (specifically, an electrical mechanism) that’s applied to system components for the prevention of rust, pitting, and the corrosion of metal surfaces that are in contact with water or soil. A low-voltage current is applied to the water or soil in contact with the metal, such that the<br /></p><p>electromotive force renders the metal component cathodic. Corrosion is concentrated on the anodes instead of on the associated (and protected) water system components. This type of corrosion may occur in copper, steel, stainless steel, cast iron, and ductile iron pipes.<br /></p>
