The NT_Turn object represents a mechanism for traversing from one link to another. Each turn will be located at a node of the underlying topology<br /></p><p>and will form the bridge for a link entering the node and a link exiting the node. The use of turns allows a restricted network (one with constraints) to be represented by an unrestricted one.<br /></p><p>The use of the word "turn" does not necessarily reflect a change of direction. Going straight through an intersection is one such viable option and would be represented by a "NT_Turn." In public transport<br /></p><p>such as a rail system<br /></p><p>the "NT_Turns" are routes through "stations" connecting one incoming "train" with one outgoing "train."  NT_Turns are thus called "connections" when speaking of airport<br /></p><p>and have temporal constraints based on the availability of the "links" that is<br /></p><p>on flight times.<br /></p><p><br /></p><p><br /></p><p>{fromLink.endNode = toLink.startNode = topology}<br /></p><p>(isTraversable} implies {fromLink.isTraversable}<br /></p><p>(isTraversable} implies {toLink.isTraversable<br /></p>
